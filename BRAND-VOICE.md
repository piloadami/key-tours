## Voice Attributes

### 1. The Insider
Keyla doesn't position herself above the client or below them — she positions herself *beside* them,
sharing something real. The whole brand is built on "I know something about this city that no guide
book has."

**Evidence:**
- "informações de uma carioca que vive a cidade — que guia nenhum tem" (verbatim)
- Explicitly not certified, and frames that as a feature: "a perspective no official script contains"
- Reels show personal moments, real life, real reactions — not scripted tourist content

**In copy:** Write as if you're telling a friend about something you discovered. Never "Rio de Janeiro
offers visitors..." — always "when we passed the cathedral, I showed them something most guides miss..."

---

### 2. Culturally Layered
Keyla connects things others keep separate: a church visit becomes a thread through Afro-Brazilian
syncretism, through samba formation, through how people in Rio actually socialise. This connective
tissue is the product.

**Evidence:**
- "visitamos a catedral... sobre o sincretismo... a importância dessas religiões para o samba...
  como essas religiões influenciaram na forma como se curte o samba, em roda"
- "conseguir decodificar grande parte de uma cidade tão complexa" — stated as the core satisfaction
- "referências atuais sobre cultura e comportamento" alongside historical facts

**In copy:** Always show the connection, not just the fact. Not "we visit the cathedral" —
"the cathedral tells a story about African religions in Brazil that most visitors never hear."

---

### 3. Warmly Honest
Keyla is transparent about what she is and isn't, and turns the "isn't" into the strongest argument
for choosing her. This honesty is rare in tourism and deeply trusted by the right clients.

**Evidence:**
- "me apresento e pontuo que sou formada em moda e precisarei pesquisar para responder algumas
  perguntas, mas que tenho informações de uma carioca que vive a cidade — que guia nenhum tem"
- Voyageurs du Monde concierge endorsed her *after* witnessing this directly
- "nunca tive clientes declaradamente de direita" — she knows who she is and is honest about it

**In copy:** Don't hide the "uncertified" fact — lean into it. The honesty is the proof point.
"I'm not a certified guide. I'm something rarer." is more compelling than any credential.

## Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Lived Knowledge Over Credentials
**Core message:** A certified guide knows the dates. Keyla knows how the city actually feels.
**Evidence:** Named explicitly in positioning, reinforced by all client feedback, embedded in bio
**Frequency:** Appears in every description of the tour and every piece of feedback received
**Example:** *"informações de uma carioca que vive a cidade — que guia nenhum tem"*

**Core message:** Rio is one of the most layered cities on earth. Most tours skim the surface.
Key Tours decodes it.
**Evidence:** "conseguir decodificar uma cidade tão complexa" — Keyla herself uses this frame

---

### Pillar 3: Private, Never a Crowd
**Core message:** You'll never be on a bus with 30 strangers. Key Tours is intimate by design.
**Evidence:** Pricing caps at 6 people. Pickup from your hotel. Restaurant together. Driver included.
**Frequency:** Structural — it's in every tour format
**Example:** Door-to-door, 1–6 guests, 5 hours, the guide eats with you.

---

### Pillar 4: Culture as Living, Not Archived
**Core message:** Key Tours doesn't present Rio's culture as a museum exhibit — as something that
*was*. It shows how history lives in the present: in samba circles, in syncretism, in architecture,
in how people move through the city today.
**Evidence:** "referências atuais sobre cultura e comportamento" listed alongside historical context
**Frequency:** Explicit in every tour description and in how Keyla describes her own approach

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- **Write in first person as Keyla.** "I show you..." not "Key Tours offers..."
- **Use PT for warmth, EN for reach.** Bilingual moments feel authentic, not translated.
  Example: "No centro da cidade — in the heart of Rio — I'll show you something most guides miss."
- **Name the connection, not just the content.** "The church and the samba circle are the same story."
- **Be specific about what makes it personal.** Door-to-door pickup. Restaurant together. My knowledge, not a script.
- **Be honest about the "uncertified" angle — briefly.** One clear sentence explaining what she is instead: "I studied fashion, not tourism — which means I know the city through its people, its culture, its contradictions." Then move on.
- **Use the client's own words as much as possible.** "Decoding Rio" came from a client — use it.
- **Moderate, purposeful emojis.** 🍃🙌🏾✨ — organic, not corporate cheerful

### Don't:
- **Don't lead with logistics.** "5-hour tour, pickup included, R$900" comes after the experience, not before.
- **Don't list stops as the main sell.** The itinerary is context. The connection between things is the product.
- **Don't use authoritative / expert positioning.** "Expert guide" sounds wrong. "Carioca who lives it" sounds right.
- **Don't over-explain the "uncertified" angle.** One honest line is enough — don't dwell on it or apologise for it.
- **Don't target everyone explicitly.** Copy naturally filters *in* curious, culturally open clients without shutting the door on anyone.

## Visual Identity

### Colour Palette
Derived from @metta.wear (Keyla's fashion label — same aesthetic sensibility):

| Role | Colour | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Earthy green | Forest, tropical, natural — not neon green |
| Secondary | Warm terracotta / orange | Brazilian warmth, sun, craft |
| Neutral | Natural beige / warm cream | Breathable, grounded, artisanal |
| Dark | Warm brown / near-black | Depth, not cold corporate black |
| Accent | Soft gold / yellow-green | Optional, for highlights |

**Never:** Electric blue, cold grey, stock-tourism teal, white-on-white sterile palette.
**Feel:** Like a well-made linen shirt, not a travel app.

### Typography Feel
- **Primary:** Warm serif or humanist sans-serif — something with personality, not system fonts
- **Secondary:** Clean, readable for body copy
- **Avoid:** Rounded display fonts that feel startup-y; slab serifs that feel heavy/formal
- **Reference:** Think along the lines of a small independent publisher or a Brazilian design studio — not a hotel chain

### Photography Style
- Natural light, golden hour, real people in motion
- Candid over posed — Keyla's reels (70–188 likes) outperform static posts by 2–3×
- Architecture and culture *through* human experience, not isolated monument shots
- Warm grade — not oversaturated, not bleached
- Never: stock photography, generic "tourist looking at landmark" shots

### Content Format Priorities (from @keylario data)
1. **Reels** — highest engagement by far (2–3× statics). Should be primary format.
2. **Carousels** — good for tour storytelling (multi-image, connected narrative)
3. **Statics** — lower engagement, use sparingly for quotes/announcements
4. **Stories** — daily personality, logistics, behind-the-scenes
