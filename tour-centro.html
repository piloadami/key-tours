City Centre & Santa Teresa Centro & Santa Teresa Centro & Santa Teresa

The heart of the city, where Rio's contradictions make sense. O coração da cidade, onde as contradições do Rio fazem sentido. El corazón de la ciudad, donde las contradicciones de Río cobran sentido.

Rio de Janeiro was born in this neighbourhood. And Keyla invites you to look at these places through a decolonial lens — the port where enslaved Africans arrived, the square where the Emperor held court, the streets where capoeira was practiced in secret beneath the gaze of the church.

The Carioca Aqueduct, two rows of elegant stone arches completed in 1750, once carried water from the Santa Teresa hills down to the city. Today it carries the iconic yellow tram. Walk through the old financial district and you'll find Belle Époque facades sitting beside Modernist towers beside colonial churches — many years of history compressed into a few walkable blocks.

The hill that escaped time

Santa Teresa is a neighbourhood of suspended time. The bohemian hill above the city where artists, poets, and musicians settled throughout the 20th century — drawn by the views, the cool air, and the sense that the rules of the city below didn't quite apply up here. Its cobbled streets wind past colonial mansions, past small studios and neighbourhood bars, past mirantes where the whole sweep of the bay opens up below you.

"Santa Teresa is where I take people when I want them to understand Rio — not the postcard version, the real one. There are places I've been going to since I was a teenager. The view from Parque das Ruínas — you see everything from there. I always end up staying longer than planned."
— Keyla, your guide
