# Business Profile — Key Tours
Date: 2026-03-27
Lang: PT/EN

---

## What the Business Does
Key Tours offers private guided cultural tours of Rio de Janeiro, led by Keyla — a native
Carioca. Tours are 5 hours, door-to-door (pickup at hotel/Airbnb included), and focus on
free attractions (museums, historic sites, viewpoints) with a culturally rich narrative that
blends history, religion, syncretism, architecture, and contemporary Carioca life. Tours end
at a restaurant with cultural relevance; clients pay for their own meal plus Keyla's.
Motorista (driver) is always part of the team. 4 routes offered:
- **City Centre / Santa Teresa** — history, Afro-Brazilian culture, geography overview (Keyla's favourite)
- **Rio Nature** — Tijuca Forest, waterfalls, panoramic trails
- **Copacabana** — modernist architecture, neighbourhood history, bohemian soul
- **Night Life** — Lapa, live music, bars, local rhythms and culture

Geography: Rio de Janeiro. Seasonal business (high season = Brazilian summer/carnival/holidays).
Language: primarily English (European/American clientele).

---

## Target Client
International tourist — European or American, culturally curious, enjoys walking, genuinely
interested in Brazil beyond the postcard. Typically a 3-star traveller (not luxury, not backpacker).
Values depth over checklists. Not attached to formal certification — wants a real Carioca's
perspective, not an official script. Politically progressive or at minimum open-minded (Keyla's
worldview flavours the tour and acts as a natural filter).

**Best client archetype:** A 35–55-year-old European who has been to many cities and is bored
of standard tours. Wants to understand a place, not just photograph it. Will refer friends
and family when they visit Rio.

**Anti-client:** Tourists who measure quality by credentials or dates. Groups that want
quantity of stops over depth of content.

with, benchmarked against a European/American 3-star client profile. Not cost-derived.
Margin appears healthy (~56–65%) on direct bookings.

takes 20% — this must be factored into pricing if that channel launches.
At 20% commission: R$900 → R$720 net. Contribution drops to ~R$320 (36%). Consider
raising Airbnb Experience price to R$1,100–1,200 to maintain margin.

## Acquisition Channels

1. **Voyageurs du Monde** — French premium travel agency. English-language tours only.
   Volume unknown, but consistent. Subcontract model (Keyla works for their clients).
2. **Vamos Boca / Vamos Rio** — Local agency, focus on stadium/sports tours.
   Lower cultural alignment but steady volume.
3. **European friend referrals** — Organic word-of-mouth. Friends of past clients.
   Highest quality channel (client already pre-sold). Zero cost.

**All clients since September 2026 came from these three sources.**

Primary channel: Agency subcontracts (reliable but not owned).
Best client source: Referrals (highest intent, best fit, zero cost).
Owned digital channels: **None.**

Owner-dependency: **Yes — 100%.** Keyla is both the product and the only sales channel.
If agencies reduce volume and referrals dry up, revenue goes to zero.

## Risks

**Primary risk:** Zero owned acquisition channels.
Voyageurs du Monde and Vamos Boca can reduce volume or drop Keyla at any time. There is
no email list, no Instagram audience, no Google presence, no direct booking page. If both
agencies pull back simultaneously, revenue hits zero within weeks.

**Motorista dependency:** Keyla identified this herself. In low season, if she's not running
frequent tours, preferred drivers deprioritise her. This creates a vicious cycle: fewer tours
→ less driver loyalty → worse experience → fewer referrals.

**Owner dependency:** Keyla is the product. If she's sick, on holiday, or unavailable, the
business doesn't operate. No backup guide. Nothing documented.

**Seasonal floor unknown:** High-season volume (8–15 tours) is confirmed. Low-season floor
is undocumented. Without that number, the annual income picture is incomplete.

**Moto tour liability:** Tours conducted by motorbike carry client safety risk. An accident
would cause severe reputational damage and possible legal exposure. No insurance mentioned.

**Mitigation (current):**
- Agency relationships provide baseline volume
- Referrals from European clients provide quality pipeline
- No formal mitigation plan for any of the above risks

## Profile Summary

Key Tours is a one-woman private cultural tour operation in Rio de Janeiro with a genuinely
strong product and clear differentiator: Keyla offers the living knowledge of a native Carioca
with a progressive, culturally rich lens — validated by premium agency Voyageurs du Monde and
consistent referral flow from past clients. The business model is lean and margin-healthy on
paper, but structurally fragile: 100% of clients come through two agencies and word-of-mouth,
with no owned digital presence. The immediate opportunity is to convert the product quality
and verbal referral pattern into owned channels — starting with Airbnb Experience and hotel
concierge partnerships — to build a low-season floor and reduce agency dependency. The 90-day
priority is less about marketing sophistication and more about claiming the channels that already
exist for a business like this.
